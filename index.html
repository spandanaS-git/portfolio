Satellite to Map Image Translation

Objective: Convert complex, raw satellite imagery into meaningful, structured map representations using advanced deep learning architectures and LLMs, primarily tested on the HugGAN Maps dataset.


Methodology & Results:

  • U-Net: Achieved the best overall performance with an SSIM score of 0.8271 and the lowest MSE (0.00127). The encoder-decoder skip connections proved vital for retaining high-resolution boundaries.
  • SegNet & Pix2Pix: SegNet produced smoother outputs but lacked sharp details. Pix2Pix (cGAN) successfully learned the overall layout but introduced noise and adversarial instability (SSIM: 0.7442).
  • Gemini 2.5 Flash (Zero-Shot): An experimental run showed that while multimodal LLMs offer immense flexibility and create visually appealing map interpretations, they fall short of specialized CNNs on strict pixel-aligned reconstruction metrics.

Medical QA using Transformers & LLMs

Objective: Build a system capable of accurately answering complex medical multiple-choice questions natively sourced from the Hugging Face MedQA dataset (US Medical Licensing Exams).


Methodology & Results:

  • Domain-Specific Models: Fine-tuned BioBERT and BioGPT models natively specialized for biomedical data. However, they struggled with the intense reasoning and instruction-following requirements of clinical MCQs, plateauing at ~26-30% accuracy.
  • Large Language Models: Pivoted to evaluating zero-shot prompting on generalized, foundational LLMs to test for latent reasoning capability.
  • Conclusion: Foundational LLMs drastically outperformed the domain-specific models. GPT-3.5-Turbo achieved ~59% accuracy, while newer models (GPT-4o-mini and Gemini-2.0-Flash-lite) reached over 75% accuracy, proving that advanced reasoning capacity is significantly more critical than domain-specific token vocabulary for clinical diagnostics.

Brain Tumor Classification & Segmentation

Objective: Automate the complex procedure of detecting abnormal brain tissues in MRI scans to save diagnostic time for radiologists and improve screening reliability (Published in ICEARS 2022).


Methodology & Results:

  • Preprocessing & Feature Extraction: Utilized Median filtering for noise removal, alongside Dual-Tree Complex Wavelet Transform (DT-CWT) and GLCM for precise texture and feature isolation.
  • Fuzzy Segmentation: Implemented Spatial Fuzzy C-Means (SFCM) clustering to robustly extract edge information and segment the tumor topologies.
  • Classification Success: Evaluated both Probabilistic Neural Networks (PNN) and Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN). CNN achieved the highest accuracy of 88.3% compared to PNN's 83.6%, demonstrating superior implicit feature learning for accurate multi-class tumor differentiation (Glioma, Meningioma, Pituitary).
